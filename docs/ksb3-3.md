# :lucide-users: KSB 3.3 (B)

!!! abstract "Requirement"

    Inspire and motivate others to deliver excellent technical solutions and outcomes.

## Evidence Details
All four members of the technical team (including myself) have worked in the organisation several years. Over this time, each individual has developed specialised knowledge in particular areas, much of which is complex and not easily documented. An example of this is the interconnectivity of our telephony components, which operate together as a unified system from the user’s perspective.

At the same time, the Service Desk team manages a high volume of requests and incident reports, often without detailed familiarity with the underlying systems or applications. This lack of visibility can make it challenging for them to fully understand or accurately diagnose issues, as well as appreciate the impact issues have on our users and members of the public.

After handling several escalated service requests and observing interactions between teams, I identified that this concentration of knowledge within the Technical Team was creating silos. This was limiting both teams’ effectiveness and ultimately impacting the quality and efficiency of service delivery to users.

To address this, I proposed the introduction of regular “skills and knowledge sharing” sessions to leadership. These sessions involve a rotating presenter from both teams, delivering in-depth overviews of specific systems or topics. Each session covers system functionality, how users interact with it and common issues and solutions.

As a result of this initiative, both the Technical and Service Desk teams are now better equipped to manage user requests and incidents. There is an improved shared understanding of systems, clearer insight into user impact, and a stronger starting point for investigation. This has enhanced collaboration between teams and enabled a more efficient, responsive, and higher-quality service for our users.

## Reflection
Although I have traditionally lacked confidence in public speaking, I found that I genuinely enjoyed delivering these sessions. Having worked with many of the applications for nearly 18 years, I have developed a significant depth of knowledge. However, much of this expertise existed as tacit knowledge rather than being formally documented. Preparing for and delivering these presentations provided a valuable opportunity to structure and document this information, while also sharing it in an engaging and interactive way with colleagues.
This process not only supported my own development but also contributed to the wider team’s capability. Since initiating and delivering these sessions, I have observed a clear improvement in the quality of first-line investigations carried out by the Service Desk team. Additionally, there has been a noticeable reduction in the number of queries directed to me from within the Technical Team, indicating increased confidence and autonomy across both teams.
